# L0-AC-VISION — Auto-Claude

**Auto-Claude** is a project-agnostic, self-improving, closed-loop software-development platform on which the human steers and the system runs the lifecycle.

**Why:** A reasoning engine can reason about code, but it cannot — on its own — run tests, enforce budgets, isolate workspaces, review its own output independently, learn from its mistakes, decide what is worth building next, or judge when a change is too risky to ship without a human. The platform provides all of that, end to end: it turns a reasoning engine into a full software-development organization — product ownership, architecture, implementation, review, quality assurance, security, compliance, delivery, and operations — running as an honest, agile, verifiable loop. Without it, the engine is a copilot that needs constant supervision. With it, the human stops *executing* the lifecycle and starts *steering* it: setting direction, approving the few decisions that genuinely need judgment, and otherwise staying out of the loop.

The deeper purpose is attention. **The human is the scarce resource.** Every routine review, every spec draft, every status check the human performs is leverage spent on work the system could carry. The platform's job is to protect the human's attention — to absorb the predictable work, surface only the decisions that truly need a human, and **ask less over time** as it learns. It learns two ways: from the skills and instructions the human improves, and from the human's own behavior — which decisions they make the same way every time, which findings they always dismiss, which work they always approve. What the system can predict, it stops asking about, while never crossing the boundaries below.

**For:** A human Operator who steers a fleet of work — authoring high-level direction, defining what is worth building, answering the decisions the system escalates, and approving production releases. The Operator defines *what* and *why*; the platform handles *how*. The Operator's interface is a calm steering surface, not a stream of pull requests to review. The platform earns the Operator's trust gradually — it starts fully human-gated and widens its own autonomy only as its quality is proven, per risk class.

**Project-agnostic by construction:** The platform is **one platform, many deployments**. A *deployment* is a project the platform runs — its own configured profile: the repositories it owns, its risk-classification rules, its compliance persona-set, its budget, its release path, its rollout slot, and **its own honest boundary of what can and cannot be automated**. The first deployment is a regulated regulated platform; it is deployment #1, not the platform's shape. A second is a product project; a third is a content website. Adding a project is registering a new deployment profile — not building a new platform. The platform's behavior is uniform; everything domain-specific lives in the deployment's profile.

**The five-layer lifecycle (with a cross-cutting compliance lane):**
- **Strategy & Research** — the human, sparring with the platform, sets direction and frames what is worth doing. (The layer that stays most human.)
- **Spec & Plan** — direction becomes layered specifications and an implementation plan, with the spec guardians enforcing layer separation and the human approving spec content.
- **Execution** — the autonomous daemon detects ready work, classifies it, decomposes it into parallel units, and drives the pipeline through implementation; it coordinates batches of concurrent work across a deployment's repositories with dependency-aware merge ordering.
- **Verification** — independent, heterogeneous review (never self-certification), holdout validation against scenarios the agent never saw, static-analysis enforcement, and end-to-end checks after delivery. A hard rule: **no verification means no eligibility to merge.**
- **Compounding** — the learning loops that make the next unit of work easier: capturing pitfalls and injecting them into future sessions, promoting recurring patterns into permanent guidance and structural guards, and keeping the canonical specs and documents honest so agents never act on a stale or contradicted instruction.
- **Compliance lane (cross-cutting)** — a per-deployment set of domain reviewers (one deployment may need privacy, medical-device-boundary, and sector-certification checks; another needs only general data-protection; a third needs none) wired as *enforced gates*, not advice. The lane defines which changes a deployment may never auto-ship.

**What the platform provides:**
- **Orchestration & execution** — work detection, complexity classification, parallel decomposition, the pipeline from implementation through delivery, and dependency-aware coordination of concurrent work across a deployment's repositories.
- **Containment** — isolated workspaces, structural access controls, cost circuit breakers, and isolation of credentials from the reasoning sessions.
- **Quality gates** — independent heterogeneous review, holdout validation, static-analysis enforcement; reviewers operate adversarially, assuming the change is flawed until proven otherwise.
- **Product co-ownership** — a bounded product-owner role that synthesizes signals (pipeline gaps, delivery health, stale work, the human's ideas) into a re-ranked backlog of proposals and *suggests* the next most valuable work. It may propose specifications; it never edits the vision. Proposals are surfaced for the human to pull (globally and in context), not pushed; all work still begins only on the human's word.
- **Technical leadership** — monitors code health, detects spec-versus-code drift, identifies failure patterns and dependency risks, and proposes improvements — always flowing through product ownership for priority before reaching the human.
- **Learning** — the compounding loops above, plus a learn-from-the-operator loop: the platform observes which decisions the Operator makes consistently and adapts how much it escalates, within hard guardrails, so it asks less about the predictable and reserves the human's attention for the genuinely novel.
- **Interactive sessions** — any role that runs autonomously in the daemon is also available as a live collaborator. The Operator can open a conversation with the product owner about priorities, with the tech lead about a technical call, or with any role; the role brings its current state into the conversation and can act on decisions on the spot. Same identity, same tools, same shared state — a different mode, not a different agent.
- **Earned-trust delivery** — a risk-class ramp drives the split between what ships autonomously and what the human decides. Low-risk changes can earn their way to autonomous merge on a clean pipeline; higher-risk changes — and anything touching authentication, sensitive data, migrations, or regulated paths — are forced to a human decision by construction, which is also the compliance lane's first line. Trust is *earned per risk class on each deployment*, never granted at switch-on: a deployment begins fully human-gated and widens autonomy only after its gates are proven.

**The steering surface — how the human interacts:**
- **One pane.** The Operator steers from a single dashboard — calm, glance-and-steer, the platform's primary interface — backed by deep-work conversational sessions when a decision needs discussion. Routine pipeline churn never reaches the Operator; only genuine decisions do.
- **A decision inbox.** Every pause that needs a human choice arrives as a uniform, self-contained decision request: the precise question, just enough context to decide, the options with a recommendation, the consequence of leaving it unanswered, and how reversible it is. A single answer transparently continues the exact work that was waiting on it. The inbox is the one cross-deployment surface: while the Operator is focused on one deployment, only sufficiently important items from the others break through; the rest accrue quietly. Priority is explainable and visible — never a hidden global ranking.
- **Coordinator, not executor.** The steering surface and the product-owner role *coordinate* — they create and shape work, set focus, answer decisions, and suggest specifications. They never merge, never deploy, never alter a pipeline phase, and never edit the vision.

**What the reasoning engine provides:** Reasoning within the boundaries the platform sets — decomposition, implementation, review, conflict resolution, diagnosis, and the judgment that the platform's roles encode.

**Boundaries (hard, load-bearing):**
- Never decides safety-critical, sensitive-data, specification-content, or production-release questions autonomously — these always reach the Operator.
- The learn-from-the-operator loop may reduce how often it asks the Operator **only** for decision-classes outside that always-escalate set; it can never learn its way past a hard boundary. The questions above are always escalated, no matter how predictable the Operator's behavior becomes.
- Never deploys to production, and never graduates a deployment to wider autonomy, without the Operator's approval; trust widens only along the earned risk-class ramp.
- Never acts on self-generated proposals without the Operator's approval — all work begins on the human's word.
- Never autonomously *finalizes* specification content — the Operator approves it — and never authors or edits the vision (L0). The platform may draft, and the product owner may *suggest*, lower-layer specs under guardrails; deciding their content is always the Operator's.
- Never modifies its own implementation or its own evaluation and approval criteria.
- The coordinator never merges, never deploys, never alters a pipeline phase, and never edits the vision.
- All permanent knowledge changes — promoting a pattern, adapting a learned behavior, changing shared guidance — happen within these guardrails; sensitive knowledge is never auto-promoted, and a bad learned bias or a bad shared change must be reversible across the fleet.
- An honest boundary of what cannot be automated is a **per-deployment property, not the platform's**: each deployment carries its own map of what is automatable (green), what strains (yellow), and what is irreducibly human (red). The platform optimizes for concentrating the Operator's scarce attention on each deployment's irreducibly-human work — it does not pretend that work away.

**Success:** The Operator steers from one calm surface and is reached only by decisions that genuinely need them — a risky change, a specification suggestion, an ambiguous finding. Predictable pipeline work lands autonomously within each deployment's earned risk class; the system measurably asks the Operator less over time for decision-classes it has learned, while never crossing the boundaries above. A recurring mistake gets structurally prevented from recurring. Adding a project is a deployment-registry entry, not a fork. Every superseded plan is explicitly marked; the canonical specs have no live contradictor. And the original promise still holds: the Operator can set direction before bed and wake to working, reviewed, honestly-gated work — with confidence that cost, safety, quality, and compliance were maintained while the human's attention was spent elsewhere.
